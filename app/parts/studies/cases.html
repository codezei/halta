Case Studies 1

Reaching Rare Disease Patients for [Pharma Company D]

The Challenge:
For their new drug targeting a rare genetic disorder, [Pharma Company D] needed to recruit a specific patient demographic which was spread thin globally and challenging to reach.

Our Solution:
We employed a multi-pronged digital strategy combining geo-targeted online ads, specialized social media groups, and collaborations with rare disease advocacy platforms. Our proprietary algorithm also screened online patient communities to identify potential candidates.

The Result:
Within just three months, we managed to recruit 80% of the required patient base, speeding up the initial phase of the clinical trial and ensuring a diverse and appropriate group of participants.

Case Studies 2

Overcoming Cultural Barriers for [Pharma Company E]

The Challenge:
Pharma Company E] was launching a trial in regions where cultural barriers and lack of awareness about clinical trials made patient recruitment difficult.

Our Solution:
Recognizing the power of community influencers, we initiated a campaign where regional micro-influencers educated their followers about the benefits and safety of clinical trials. Parallelly, we developed multilingual digital platforms to answer queries, address concerns, and simplify the registration process for interested candidates.

The Result:
The influencer campaign reached over a million individuals, leading to a 50% surge in patient inquiries. Eventually, patient recruitment goals were met two months ahead of schedule.

Case Studies 3

Optimizing Retention for [Pharma Company F]

The Challenge:
While [Pharma Company F] could initially recruit patients for their long-term study on an arthritis drug, they struggled with a high drop-out rate, endangering the trial's validity.

Our Solution:
We implemented a digital patient engagement system, offering virtual check-ins, reminders, and an interactive platform where patients could log their experiences and get immediate feedback. We also launched a monthly newsletter highlighting the trial's progress and importance, ensuring patients felt involved and valued.

The Result:
The drop-out rate plummeted by 70%. The trial not only retained its participants but also garnered a community of engaged patients eager to share their positive experiences, promoting further recruitment.
